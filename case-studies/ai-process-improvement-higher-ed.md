# AI-Enabled Process Improvement in Higher Education Operations

> **Case Study Type:** Process Improvement Design Exercise  
> **Domain:** Higher Education & Nonprofit Operations  
> **Methodologies:** Lean Six Sigma (DMAIC), AI-Augmented Workflows  
> **Date:** January 2025

---

## Context (Problem)

Administrative units in higher education and nonprofits often face:
- Manual, repetitive workflows (intake, tracking, reporting)
- Inconsistent documentation and approval bottlenecks
- Poor data visibility, delaying decisions and increasing compliance risk

These inefficiencies increase operational costs, staff burnout, and audit exposure.

---

## Approach (What I Did)

Using **Lean Six Sigma (DMAIC)** principles and AI-enabled tools, I designed a streamlined operational model for a higher-education-style administrative process.

### Methods Used

| Phase | Action |
| ------- | -------- |
| **Define** | Identified bottlenecks in simulated program intake and reporting workflow |
| **Measure** | Mapped time per step and error rates in manual tracking |
| **Analyze** | Found redundant approvals, data re-entry, and unclear ownership |
| **Improve** | Introduced GPT-based document summarization, standardized workflows, implemented automated dashboards |
| **Control** | Established SOPs and performance metrics for sustainability |

---

## Results (Estimated Impact)

- **30-40% reduction in cycle time** (based on workflow mapping)
- **Reduced manual errors** through standardized intake and AI validation
- **Improved leadership visibility** via dashboards and automated reporting
- **Staff capacity shift** from data entry to higher-value analysis

*Developed as a continuous improvement design exercise using real-world Lean Six Sigma and AI methodologies. Framework applicable to higher education, nonprofit, and federal program operations.*
